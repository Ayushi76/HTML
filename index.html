About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the southern bank
of the river Yamuna in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632
by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favourite wife, Mumtaz Mahal;
it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.
